Notable Facts About Joanne Rowling:

  • Joanne wanted to be a writer from an early age. She wrote her first book around the age of six.
  • She studied for a year in Paris while attending Exeter University. Her strong knowledge of French and Classics were the tools to help her create Harry Potter.
  • Joanne conceived the idea for her Harry Potter books from sitting on a delayed train. From then, in 1990 to 1995, she mapped out all seven books in the series.
  • Joanne was turned down many times when trying to publish her book. But when one literary agent wrote back and asked for more she said it was, "the best letter I had ever received in my life."
  • Because her publisher thought a book by an obvious female would not appeal to the target audience of young boys, Joanne shortened her name to J. K. Rowling (the "K" stands for Kathleen, her paternal grandmother's name).
